### Block/SOW Structure

- Each block is prepaid for agreed number of hours
- SOW details proposed work for that block
- SOW is "intended" but not guaranteed - work may take longer/shorter
- New items can be added by client or developer
- When hours are exhausted, new block + revised SOW created
- **Benefits**: Agile, fair to both parties. Client can influence direction quickly. Developer underestimates not penalized. Developer overestimates don't penalize client.
- **Payment timing**: Paid at time of invoice (or day after) - not formalized
- **Payment method**: Wise transfer (Brian's Wise account â†’ Fraser's Canadian Wise account)

## Concerns & Risks

### Developer's Identified Concerns

1. **Personal naming vs corporate protection**: Named personally as implementer - worried this breaches corporate veil protection of being an employee of The Technifar Corporation

2. **Notification addresses missing**: Agreement requires written notifications but provides no addresses

3. **Email notification preferred**: Would prefer notifications via email with proof of receipt (reply or similar mechanism)

4. **SOW structure**: Would prefer SOWs be more meaningful, with each SOW superseding the previous one

5. **Liability exceptions too broad**: The exceptions to liability cap and the maximum dollar exposure need to be removed or limited

6. **Non-compete willingness**: Willing to agree not to compete with this product in the future - wants client to feel secure, but unsure if this helps or hurts developer position

7. **Section 7(b) - 24-month warranty problem**: Requires free defect correction for 24 months post-acceptance. Problem: Cannot determine if issues arose from delivered code vs. new code/services introduced later. Burden of proof concern.

8. **Indefinite survival clauses (5e, 6a, 8d)**: Obligations survive "indefinitely" after agreement ends. Is this fair/standard?

9. **Section 11 - Governing law/venue**: Louisiana law, Baton Rouge courts - seems one-sided for Canadian developer

10. **Sections 12a, 12b - Understanding needed**: Unclear what counterpart execution and recitals incorporation mean

11. **Missing developer protections**: What should be added from developer's perspective?
