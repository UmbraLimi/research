## The Six Core Hypotheses (MUST VALIDATE IN MVP)

Our entire business model depends on validating these assumptions:

1. **Market Positioning Hypothesis**
   - Students will pay tutoring prices ($300-600 for a course)
   - Test: Do Genesis students actually pay?

2. **Completion Rates Hypothesis**
   - We'll achieve 75%+ completion (vs 10-20% for MOOCs)
   - Test: How many Genesis students finish the course?

3. **Customer Segmentation Hypothesis**
   - We'll attract BOTH "Earn-While-Learn" students AND "Premium Learner-Only" students
   - Test: What ratio of each type enrolls?

4. **Conversion to Teaching Hypothesis**
   - 10%+ of students will become Student-Teachers
   - Test: How many Genesis students choose to teach?

5. **Peer Teaching Quality Hypothesis**
   - Student-Teachers can match expert outcomes
   - Test: Do students taught by Student-Teachers learn as well?

6. **Flywheel Validation Hypothesis**
   - Second generation emerges (Student-Teachers recruit new students)
   - Test: Do Student-Teachers actually recruit and teach?

**Brian's Uncertainty Ranking (from Session 1):**

## Decision-Making Framework for MVP Spec

When deciding if a feature should be in MVP, ask:

### MUST HAVE (Include in MVP):
- Does it DIRECTLY validate one of the six hypotheses?
- Is it CRITICAL for Genesis cohort to enroll and pay?
- Can we NOT test our model without it?
- Would students lose trust if it's missing?

**Examples:**
- Payment processing (validates Hypothesis #1)
- Course content delivery (validates Hypothesis #2)
- Student-Teacher matching (validates Hypothesis #4)
- Progress tracking (validates Hypothesis #5)

### NICE TO HAVE (Consider, but not critical):
- Does it improve the experience but isn't required for validation?
- Can we work around it manually for Genesis cohort?
- Would it help retention but not affect initial enrollment?

**Examples:**
- Automated scheduling (Brian can schedule manually)
- Community forum (can use Discord instead)
- Advanced analytics (Brian can track in spreadsheets)

### OUT OF SCOPE (Save for Phase 2):
- Is it optimization for scale (not needed for 30 students)?
- Is it a "nice to have" for future users?
- Does it add complexity without validating hypotheses?

**Examples:**
- Multiple creators/courses (start with Gabriel/Guy's courses only)
- Mobile app (web-first is fine)
- Automated quality control (Brian reviews manually)
- Marketing automation (Brian recruits manually)
